Spotlight on Politics!

You’d think something as important to a territory’s future as politics wouldn’t be as crazy or confusing. To help you navigate the chaos, here are some tips to help you find the way to the truth!

Tip 1: Don't Believe Gossip

Gossip is something that preys on our minds, its palatability more welcome to us than the simplicity of cold information. While there may be a little truth in the gossip, a lot of it is most likely imagined to fill the gaps between the bits of actual truth. As intriguing and interesting as it may be, don’t believe anything without solid proof. In the absence of facts, we can only fill in the gaps, but it is important to remember that our imagined truth is not truth until it is proven. It is merely speculation, nothing more.

Tip 2: Beware Of Opinions

Opinions are something to be wary of when you are looking for news about politics and politicians. There are articles and posts where it is pretty evident that what is being said is just the opinion of the creator, but there are some where it this information is rather vague. Because of that, always remember to fact-check your information. However, it is not just other people’s opinions you should be wary of, but also your own. Some people have a tendency to dismiss information as untrue or vice versa just because it goes against or coincides with their political opinions. Remember that when getting updated as to what is the real situation, one must accept all truth and only truth wholeheartedly. Do not protect your opinion from the truth, but use the truth to shape your opinions.

Tip 3: Groups = Biased

Politics has a way of tearing the common folk apart in a flurry of disgust and disapproval towards each other’s opinions. It brings people with like opinions together and tears those with different opinions apart. This is evident in the springing up of multiple groups in platforms such as Facebook dedicated to those political opinions. Considering the fact that these groups bring together people with common opinions, you can expect that news (or even information) that comes from these groups will be biased towards the person or party the members support. In fact, some pieces of information may be put there by infiltrators who have a differing political opinion in order to see how many people will fall for the incorrect information. In other words, information that comes from groups like these are usually just bits of information, handpicked and filtered, that strive to strengthen the opinions of the members that see them. They often hide the truth or frame it in a way that makes it seem appealing. Do not rely on groups like these for information.
