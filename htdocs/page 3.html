Spotlight on the Pandemic!

Like many issues of world importance, everyone has stuff to say about the COVID-19 pandemic. But in this great collection of information, is all of it correct? Nope, but here are some tips to find out what is.

Tip 1: Don’t Believe All You See

From the start of the pandemic, many speculations have been circulating online about how the virus can be cured. Unfortunately, not all of these are actually true! You will learn many things from social media, search engines or even your next-door neighbors but keep in mind that not all of these pieces of information come from experts. Here’s a quote from Arlene Chai’s Eating Fire and Drinking Water that captures this tip: “So listen all you like, but disbelieve all you hear… You are in the city of lies.”

Tip 2: Only Trust Experts

The only valid information about the virus is the information that comes from experts who have studied it. Only people who have done extensive research about it are allowed to give confirmation about whether something works or doesn’t with regards to COVID-19. Information from other people should only be believed if this information originally came from the results of proper researches made by people qualified to make them. You should note that influential figures in either society or just in your life such as actors, vloggers, politicians, relatives, friends and neighbors are not considered experts. Because of this, original information or information sourced from actual experts but interpreted in a manner that means something different is not accurate and should not be believed.

Tip 3: Interpret Properly

Let’s say you want to share to a family member some new information you learned about the virus. So you got your information from a valid source? That’s good! You’re certain this source got its information from an expert? That sounds great! Are you a hundred percent sure that you will be able to communicate accurate information to a family member? Well, not completely. How come? Are you sure that you interpreted the information correctly? If you didn’t and your interpretation is different from message that the original information wants to portray, you yourself might end up spreading fake news! Unfortunately, not everyone who tries their best to disseminate correct information watches out for this, which is something you have to watch out for when hunting for information. You may get too comfortable noting that a piece of information has a proper source without finding out that the information and the source have conflicting messages! That is why you should always confirm with the original source before believing news you see on the internet.
